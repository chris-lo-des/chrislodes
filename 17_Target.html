Target

Over a six-month engagement, SYP helped Target develop a framework for culture that could bridge corporate and field employees with shared language around vision, values, and leadership. As part of the rollout, we created digital and physical tools which reinforced the new culture. This included 800 custom-made kits for a virtual leadership meeting and 2,000 “culture kits” sent to leaders of all Targets retail locations and distribution centers, touching 350,000 employees.

As creative lead on the project, I guided all visual output for both phases of work. This included experience design for key meetings and events, visual direction for the culture framework launch, and collaboration with clients and vendors for design and production of both physical kits.

《 Context 》

Target has a long history of design, with pioneering approaches to designer partnerships and a portfolio of unique internal brands. To create something accompanying Target's new vision for culture, we wanted to push into something future-facing that would still feel drawn from core identity elements.

The design process thus began with visual research, synthesizing the heart of what made something feel ‘Target.’ In ensuing weeks, we tried to draw from this research to express the culture vision “we care, grow, and win together.”

《 ideation and Exploration 》

Pretty quickly, I settled on a visual system that used small illustrated "fruit stickers" as central visual in each composition. I reached out to some talented illustrators from the church community and commissioned a weekly sticker, each themed around a related verse. Each ilustration would include lettering for the fruit of the week, references to related Scipture, and any illustration. Prompting and feedback was as loose as possible to maintain a diversity of visual elements and styles.

Creating a neutral backdrop and type system turned out to be quite challenge. I needed something that wouldn't draw too much attention away from ther sticker, but still successfully communicated the theme. I experimented with a number of different type treatments and background styles inspired by fruiit and food labeling, but quickly found that anyuthing too literal or graphic ended up pretty distracting. Blank color fields in a digital environment also proved dimensionless in a digital context, and there were only so many free macro fruit pictures I could find online…


The final concept draws from circles and patterns of Target to express key components of the new culture we were trying to reinforce. Die-cut holes suggest how the framework is a reframing focus on reinforcing existing Target behaviors, while a pattern of growing dots serves as both literal expression of the Vision and a reference to a Target cart design.


We used this approach and visual elements for various deliverables over the course of the second phase, including physical kits, digital leadership tools, and presentations for meetings with internal stakeholders.

A selection of final files and mockups is shown on the next page.


The most complicated deliverable was a kit sent to 800 key leaders for a virtual summit we helped design. We developed design the box’s contents, and helped brainstorm relevant gifts and tools sent to leaders.

A key aspect of our designed experience was a moment of “culture sightings,” where we’d ask leaders to take five minutes to write a physical or virtual letter to a team member they’d noticed living this value. To support this, we designed and produced a small stationery kit with branded notecards, stickers, and pre-stamped envelopes.

The kit also included a letter from the CEO, a glass dome “magnifier” (as memento for the idea of “culture sightings”), a small booklet of prompts used during the session, and a selection of snacks from Target-supported Black-owned businesses.
